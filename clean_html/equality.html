Education equality

Over the perior of 28 years, we have seen increase in number of students applying for college. We used the enthicity breakdown for bachelor degree enrollment in US, and found a significant increase in enrollment of minority groups such as hispanic, asians, and black enthic groups. Of all enthnic groups, American Indians and Alaskan Natives have not grown much compared to other ethnic groups.

In terms of education inequality, we used the education gini coeffecient value. Gini coeffecient is a normalized value with the cumulative percentage of the income on the vertical axis, and the cumulative percentage of the population on the horizontal axis. World bank document of the detail gini coeffecient calculation can be found in the link. The line at 45 degrees represents perfect equality of education. The Gini coefficient can then be thought of as the ratio of the area that lies between the line of equality and the Lorenz curve (marked A in the diagram) over the total area under the line of equality (marked A and B in the diagram); if area A is small with 0 gini coeffecient, it represent perfect equality of education. If area of A is large and have gini coeffecient of 1, it represent perfect inequality.

For the total population, the area A is apparent in the age range between age 5 and 24, where people are considered in the age of schooling. The last plot on population over 25 shows that there maybe population that did not complete the higher education resulting in inequality in number of years the population spent schooling. the wider the spread between the equiblium line to the plotted line, the larger the education year difference within the demographics will be.

When we observing the same value for female population to the total population education gini coeffecient, the gap between the equilibrium line and the scatter plot is wider than that of total population. This observation tells that there are wider inequality in number of schooling in the demographics of female than that of total populaton.

Compared to female population, the male population showed less spread between the equilibrium line to the scatter plot for most age range, however for the population above 25 years of age showed more wider spread than that of female's. We can assume that there maybe more population achieving higher education than the female population resulting in wider gender gap.

In summary, from the data we gathered, there were education inequality in the ethnicity and the gender we observed. Minority group still has lower population of applying for college, and overall population still have less years of education completion for female than that of male population.
