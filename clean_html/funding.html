Funding and effectiveness

First, when we visualized US government spenidng on enducation, we created a dashboard of the elementary and high school revenue / expendeture. Every public educational institution has three main sources of revenue; 1) local, 2) state, and 3) fecedal. We confirmed the same observation as the initial observation sited from the Our World in Data that the most revenue source is from local governmen, then the state, and least from the federal government. When observing the expendetures, majority of the spending is allocated towards structuring programs and designing course curriculum, then instructional expendeture which we compensate our faculties and staff members, then lastly to support services for studends with special needs.

For detailed ovservation, the dashboard can be used to select different years between 2000 through 2015, all 52 US states, and educaion districts. Further, we can perform state and district wise distribution and comparison of total revenue, total expendeture with a breakdown of capital and operational expendeture. The US map shows the state wise budget deficit for a given year with a heat map to show the severity of the deficit.

When we look at the public vs private college enrollment populations, the overall enrollment for both public and proviate institution between 1965 through 2015 has a steady growth over time. Using the linear regression model, we compared the predicted the future enrollment population and between our linear regression model with the projected value that had been provided from the US census.


With the projectd college enrollment population, the linear regression model to predict public and private college enrollment had mean square error and r2 of 0.408892701446 and 0.951595725635 respectively. This shows that the linear regression model has a very good fit of prediction value.

Over a period of 40 years, we see a steady growth in both public and private. The growth rate of cost of higher education for public institution is higher than that of private one.

As observed from the spending dashboard, the instructional expendeture is one of the significant factors of education spending. Faculties teaching in a private institutions are paid more than public instritution of the same level.

When we look at college education and completion rate by state, we observed that the coastal states and states around the greate lake had a higher graduation rate than other states.

From the students' perspective, even with the publicly funded institutions, many students still spend significant amount in education expenses and many borrow student loans to complete thier education. For those student who complete college education within 5 to 6 years, we plot the percentage of graduation rate over students who graduate with student loan. We observed that the highest gratuation rated states are in east coast with the exception of California. We also observed that the highest states with percent student who graduate with student loan are New Hampshire and South Dakota. There was a linear trendline that showed that the more students are graduating the higher the students with student loan we saw. We assumed that the students with access to education funding had more chance of graduating the college program.

We also observed that the average student loan amount borrowd by state for public 4 years college showed an interesting result. The highest average debpt amount to lowest state had a difference of 17k. To our suprise, California was one of the lower average student loan states amongst other state. Some of the states we observed in the previous graph that had highest rate of students with student loan had above average studnet loan amount that they took out.

The graph shows the average income of population of workers between ages 25 and 34 between 2000 through 2015. The breakdown of the average income by highest enducation level has a positive correlation between the average level of income. the higher the education, higher the income. The overall average income was very close to the average income of the associate's degree population.

Below plot shows the unemployment rate vs highest education level over 2005 to 2015. There was a negative colloration between the highest education level and the the unemplyment rate. The higher the education, the lower the unemployment rate. Irregardless the highest education level, we observed a similar unemploymnet rate change over time. We assumed that the economic environment durign the same period of time has the similar impact to the unemployment rate for the total populaiton. We can also see from the graph that having a minimu high school education significanly reduces the unemplyment rate. This shows that it is very important to obtain high school completion of education to mitigate the risk of not being employed.

The final plot shows the highschool completion rate between overall US population with the low income population by states. We observed that the difference between overall and low income level with high school completion level is insignificant between US states. We can interpret that the education spending is effective in at least providing accessable highschool education in all US states.

In summary, what we learned from observing the education revenue, expendeture, student college graduation rate, student loan, and average income information is that many education does play a significant role in the wellbeing of the citizen. Over time, the tuition and other education cost has significantly increased. US government does do a good job of providing at least secondary education to all of its citizens helping them succeed. However, when it comes to tertiary education, it is up to individual to support their own in order to get higher level degree. In order to get higher education, most of the states will require more than half of the students to take out student loans. We saw there is minor correlation between student loan amount and level of higher education they can achieve. Having higher education level does help you get higher paying job as seen by our charts. With more years of schooling, citizens can have higher paying job when economy is doing well, and are more resilient during the economic downturn.
